Understanding Concrete Grades

Concrete grades refer to the strength and composition of concrete, and the minimum strength it should have after 28 days of construction. The M in the grade refers to "Mix" and the number indicates the concrete's strength in N/mm² after 28 days.

For example, M10 means the strength of concrete after 28 days is 10 Newtons/Square Millimeter or 1,450 pounds per square inch (PSI).

1 Newton/mm² = 1 Megapascal (MPa)
